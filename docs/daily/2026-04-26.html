Passkeys don't secure the session — XSS and CSRF gaps remain post-authentication
Passkeys via WebAuthn eliminate shared secrets and phishing risk at the authentication layer, but the application session after login remains fully exposed to XSS and CSRF. Developers must still enforce CSP, SameSite cookie attributes, and step-up authentication challenges for high-risk operations — a gap that's easy to miss when passkey adoption is framed as a security silver bullet.
