Data Warehouse vs Data Lake vs Data Mesh: Trade-offs Summarised
Data warehouses enforce schema-on-write for fast, consistent queries but are slow to onboard new sources. Data lakes store raw everything (schema-on-read) but degrade into unmanageable swamps without strict governance. Data mesh distributes ownership to domain teams with shared standards — effective at scale but demands engineering maturity in every business unit.
